## SLIDE 4 — The Gender Gap

### Only 15 of 55 AU Member States Have >30% Women in Parliament

**Continental Average: 24.7%** — less than half the Agenda 2063 target of 50%

**Regional Breakdown:**

| Region | Women in Parliament (%) | Distance to Target |
|--------|:-----------------------:|:------------------:|
| Southern Africa | **30.2%** | -19.8pp |
| East Africa | **28.4%** | -21.6pp |
| West Africa | **22.1%** | -27.9pp |
| North Africa | **21.5%** | -28.5pp |
| Central Africa | **18.3%** | -31.7pp |

**Key Insight:** At current rates, most AU member states will not reach the 50% target by 2063. Southern Africa leads but is still 20 percentage points short.

**Recommendation:** Scale proven interventions from top-performing states (Rwanda 61%, South Africa 46%, Namibia 44%) to states below 15%.

## SLIDE 5 — The Youth Crisis

### Youth Unemployment Averages 17.4% — Nearly 3x the Agenda 2063 Target

**Agenda 2063 Target: <6%** | Continental Average: **17.4%**

**Critical Cases (>25% Youth Unemployment):**

| Country | Youth Unemployment | Trend |
|---------|:------------------:|:-----:|
| South Africa | **63.9%** | Worsening |
| Eswatini | **47.8%** | Stable |
| Libya | **45.2%** | Worsening |
| Gabon | **38.7%** | Stable |
| Lesotho | **34.5%** | Stable |

**The scale of the challenge:** Africa's youth population is the fastest-growing in the world. By 2063, the continent will have 830 million young people. A youth unemployment rate 3x the target is not just a statistic — it is a continental security, economic, and development crisis.

**Recommendation:** Prioritize youth employment interventions in Southern Africa (5 countries above 25%). Cross-regional knowledge sharing from states with <10% youth unemployment.

*Layout: Large "17.4%" in red center-top. Map or bar chart showing critical countries highlighted. Arrow showing gap between 17.4% and 6% target. Recommendation card at bottom.*

**GDP per Capita by Region:**

| Region | GDP per Capita | vs Continental Avg |
|--------|:-------------:|:------------------:|
| **North Africa** | **$4,123** | +47% |

| **East Africa** | **$1,087** | -61% |

**The gap within Africa is as significant as the gap to the target.** North Africa's GDP per capita is nearly 4x East Africa's. Agenda 2063 cannot succeed without targeted investment in the regions furthest behind.

**Key Comparison:** Southern Africa leads in education enrollment (68% secondary); Central Africa lags (34%). Infrastructure access follows the same pattern — internet penetration ranges from 55% (North) to 18% (Central).

## SLIDE 9 — The Platform Architecture

### Built for the Complete Data Lifecycle — Not Just Dashboards

```
Data Sources (World Bank API, UN SDG, Manual Uploads)
    ↓
ETL Pipeline (Extract, Clean, Validate, Load) — Automated
    ↓
PostgreSQL Database (13 tables, 11,700+ records)
    ↓
Insights Engine (Auto-generates findings, alerts, recommendations)
    ↓
REST API (FastAPI — 35+ endpoints with Swagger docs)
    ↓
AU-Branded Dashboard (Real-time KPIs, charts, executive reports)
```

| Layer | Technology | Why |
|-------|-----------|-----|
| Backend | Python, FastAPI | Async performance, auto-generated docs |
| Database | PostgreSQL (Supabase) | Enterprise-grade, Row-Level Security |
| ETL | World Bank API integration | Real, authoritative data for all 55 states |
| Insights | 10 generators, 6 types | Automated intelligence, not just charts |
| Frontend | HTML/JS, Plotly.js | AU-branded, interactive, responsive |

*Layout: Vertical flow diagram with icons at each step. Right side: tech stack table. AU branding throughout. Bottom: "Production-grade — Docker deployed, fully documented."*

## SLIDE 11 — Recommendations

### Three Strategic Priorities Emerging from the Data

**1. Close the Youth Employment Gap** (Critical)
- Continental youth unemployment at 17.4% — nearly 3x the 6% target
- 5 countries above 25% — require immediate, targeted intervention
- Cross-regional knowledge transfer from states with <10% unemployment
- *Impact: Address the single largest off-track Agenda 2063 indicator*

**2. Accelerate Gender Representation** (Urgent)
- Only 15 of 55 states have >30% women in parliament
- Continental average (24.7%) is less than half the 50% target
- Scale proven policies from Rwanda, South Africa, Namibia
- *Impact: Move from 49% to 75%+ of Gender Equality target by 2030*

**3. Bridge the Regional Economic Divide** (Strategic)
- East Africa GDP per capita ($1,087) is 4x lower than North Africa ($4,123)
- Infrastructure access (internet, electricity) follows the same pattern
- Targeted investment in lagging regions is essential for continental convergence
- *Impact: Reduce intra-continental inequality — a prerequisite for the $12,000 GDP target*
